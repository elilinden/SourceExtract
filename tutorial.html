How to Use SourceExtract

Learn everything you need to know in one place. Follow these four steps to browse, extract, and download media from any webpage.

1

Search & Browse

Type any website address or search term into the address bar to start browsing. SourceExtract works just like a regular browser — navigate to any webpage where you want to find images or videos.

Scroll through the page fully before extracting to ensure all lazy-loaded content is visible.
2

Extract Media

Tap the extract button to scan the current page. SourceExtract uses four detection methods — DOM parsing, video detection, CSS scanning, and network monitoring — to find every image and video on the page.

The scan runs automatically. Duplicates are filtered out so you only see unique results.
3

Select & Download

Browse your results on the Media page. Filter between images and videos, switch between grid and list view, and tap items to select them. When you're ready, tap Download to send your selected items to the download queue.

Use the "All" tab to see everything at once, or filter by Images or Videos to narrow your results.
4

Start Downloads

Your selected items appear in the Download Queue. Tap Start to begin downloading. The queue shows your progress in real time — items completed, items remaining, and any failures. When downloads finish, save them directly to your Photos library.

You can clear the queue at any time and start a new batch. Failed items can be retried individually.
